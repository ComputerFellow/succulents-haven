Desert Garden Project

Creating sustainable desert landscapes using native succulents and cacti

Explore Project

About the Project

The Desert Garden Project transforms arid landscapes into thriving ecosystems using native succulent species. Our approach focuses on water conservation and biodiversity, creating beautiful yet sustainable environments that require minimal maintenance.

This project began in 2020 in Southern California and has since expanded to five different desert communities. We've successfully converted over 50,000 square feet of traditional lawns into water-wise succulent gardens, saving millions of gallons of water annually.

Vertical Gardens

About the Project

Our Vertical Gardens project brings nature to urban environments through innovative living wall systems. These installations feature carefully selected succulent species that thrive in vertical arrangements, creating stunning natural artwork while improving air quality.

Each vertical garden is custom-designed for its location, considering sunlight exposure, climate, and architectural context. Our patented irrigation system ensures efficient water use while maintaining plant health. These living walls have transformed office buildings, restaurants, and residential spaces across the city.
